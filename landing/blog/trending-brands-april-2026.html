Top 10 Trending Brands on Rosier — April 2026

📅 Published April 1, 2026 ⏱️ 12 min read 📍 by Rosier

Every month, Rosier's community of fashion-obsessed women reveals authentic trends—not what algorithms say is trending, but what real people are actually swiping, saving, and shopping right now. April's data is fascinating. We've analyzed over 500,000 swipes, 45,000 saves, and 12,000 purchases to bring you the brands that genuinely moved the needle this month.

The data tells a story: contemporary brands are winning. Niche is beating mainstream. And authenticity is outperforming hype. Here are the 10 brands that dominated Rosier in April 2026.

1. Ganni — The Consistent Winner

Ganni holds the #1 spot for the third consecutive month. Why? Because they make contemporary pieces that actually work. Their SS26 collection of linen separates and oversized silhouettes resonated deeply with Rosier's community. The Copenhagen-based label's approach to color—soft taupes, sage greens, muted blues—aligns perfectly with the "quiet luxury" aesthetic that's dominating 2026.

What our users are saving: Structured linen shirts ($245), wide-leg trousers ($325), and their signature bucket hats ($165). The average Rosier user saves 2.3 Ganni pieces per month.

2. Deiji Studios — The New Obsession

Deiji Studios jumped from #12 last month to #2 this month. This is significant. The Australian loungewear-turned-contemporary label is winning because they're solving a real problem: women want comfort without sacrificing design. Their ribbed knit sets and bias-cut dresses feel elevated and lived-in at the same time.

Charlie, our founder, specifically requested we feature this brand. After spending time with Deiji Studios' creative direction, we understand why: they're building a coherent aesthetic that appeals to women who care deeply about proportion, texture, and sustainability.

What's moving: Their silk-blend everyday dresses ($280), oversized button-ups ($245), and the cult-favorite "Apartment Pant" ($320) that appears in 8% of saves featuring Deiji.

3. Staud — Proportion Masters

Staud's strength lies in unexpected proportions and structural design. Their April drops featured oversized blazers with cinched waists, A-line skirts in unexpected lengths, and their legendary leather goods. For a brand originally known for bags, their transition to contemporary apparel is remarkable.

Rosier data: Staud has a 34% add-to-cart rate (the highest among top-10 brands). This tells us that women who engage with Staud content follow through with purchases. It's not just aspirational—it's convertible.

4. Nanushka — The Minimalist Favorite

Nanushka dropped their capsule collection mid-April, and the timing was perfect. Their vegan leather jackets, bias-cut slip dresses, and structured knitwear fill a specific gap: investment pieces for the minimalist who values ethics. The brand's commitment to sustainable production resonates with Rosier's community of conscious consumers.

Interesting trend: Users who save Nanushka pieces tend to also save from The Row, Baserange, and COS. This suggests a specific aesthetic preference (minimalist, sustainable, investment-focused) that's worth understanding for your own shopping.

5. Khaite — The Luxury Contemporary Anchor

Khaite remains a staple, holding steady at #5. Why? Because their design philosophy is impeccable. Chloé Harite's approach to tailoring—sharp shoulders, precise cuts, quality fabrics—creates pieces that feel expensive (they are) but not ostentatious. On Rosier, Khaite pieces get saved by women looking to invest in items that will last years.

Their bestsellers this month: The "Kendrick" cashmere turtleneck ($690), slim-fit trousers ($850), and leather minimal heels ($695). Price point is high, but add-to-cart rates remain strong.

6. Sandy Liang — Micro-Influencer Made Good

Sandy Liang's ascent continues. Starting as a micro-influencer on TikTok, she's now a major contemporary designer. Her brand does what micro-influencers do best: create deeply opinionated, highly specific aesthetics. Colorful cardigans, oversized blazers, and quirky basics that feel intentional.

What's remarkable: Sandy Liang's audience skews younger (25-35) than other top brands, but their piece values remain high ($200-$500). This proves that micro-influencer aesthetics have matured into legitimate contemporary fashion.

7. Reformation — The Sustainable Standard

Reformation's April performance reflects continued growth in the sustainable luxury space. Their new deadstock fabric initiatives and vintage-inspired silhouettes attracted significant engagement. For many Rosier users, Reformation represents the entry point into considered consumption: affordable-ish, sustainable, and stylish.

Price sensitivity note: Reformation pieces are saved more frequently but converted to cart less often than higher-end brands, suggesting price is still a barrier—even though it's lower than Khaite or The Row.

8. ROTATE — The Event-Dressing Expert

ROTATE specializes in occasion wear with unexpected details—cutouts, asymmetry, unexpected proportions. Their April collection performed exceptionally well, driven largely by spring events and weddings. The Danish brand's approach to "special" dressing feels contemporary rather than formal.

Usage insight: ROTATE content has the highest save-to-purchase conversion during April-May (spring/summer events). This is valuable: if you're planning an important event in the next month, ROTATE is worth exploring.

9. Collina Strada — The Artistic Voice

Collina Strada's experimental approach to color, proportion, and textile continues to resonate with Rosier's creative community. Their April collection featured hand-painted details and innovative use of mixed fabrics. For women who view fashion as art first and wearability second, Collina Strada is magnetic.

Who loves them: Artists, designers, creative professionals. The brand skews toward self-expression over mainstream appeal, which is exactly why Rosier users appreciate it.

10. Jacquemus — The Haute Couture Playfulness

Jacquemus rounds out the top 10, maintaining momentum with their playful approach to luxury. Small sizes, unexpected proportions, bold colors—Jacquemus pieces make a statement. Their April bags (the iconic "le shoulder bag") and their new sunglasses line generated significant swipes.

The Jacquemus phenomenon: The brand polarizes users. Some save multiple pieces monthly; others never save anything. This polarization actually contributes to engagement—people have strong opinions, which drives discussion and shares.

Beyond the Top 10: Emerging Brands to Watch

Several brands moved into the top 20 this month and deserve attention. Auralee, Needles, and Visvim (Japanese contemporary menswear-inspired) are gaining momentum among Rosier users interested in technical fabrics and minimalist aesthetics. If you've been sleeping on Japanese contemporary brands, April's data suggests it's time to pay attention.

What April's Trends Tell Us About Women's Fashion in 2026

1. Comfort is non-negotiable. The rise of Deiji Studios and Baserange (both comfort-forward brands) signals that women are done sacrificing fit and feel for aesthetics. Contemporary fashion is evolving to meet this demand.

2. Sustainability is mainstream. Reformation, Nanushka, and Jacquemus (increasingly investing in sustainability) occupy 3 of the top 10 spots. Ethical production is no longer a niche concern—it's a baseline expectation.

3. Proportion matters more than branding. Staud's 34% conversion rate and Ganni's consistent top placement suggest that women respond to innovative, well-executed proportions over celebrity or mega-brand status.

4. Micro-influencer aesthetics are here to stay. Sandy Liang's position in the top 10 proves that creators can become legitimate fashion designers. The line between influencer and designer has blurred entirely.

5. Authenticity beats hype. The absence of pure hype brands from the top 10 is telling. Brands that have genuine aesthetic philosophies outperform those chasing trends.

How to Use This Data for Your Own Shopping

If you save pieces from brands in positions 1-5, explore brands in positions 6-10. The data shows significant overlap in aesthetic preferences. Women who love Ganni also appreciate ROTATE. Deiji fans often save from Reformation. Understanding these connections helps you discover your next favorite brand.

Second: If you're considering an investment piece, look at Rosier's conversion data. Brands with high add-to-cart and purchase rates (Staud, Khaite) tend to retain value and wearability long-term. Brands with high saves but lower conversion (Collina Strada, Jacquemus) are often aspirational—beautiful to save, but less likely to become daily pieces.
