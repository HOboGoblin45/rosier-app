## Email Tone Guide for Wallpaper Brand Outreach

### Do:
- **Be specific about why this brand.** Reference their aesthetic, recent collaborations, or market position
- **Lead with audience fit.** The core value is access to curated, design-conscious women—not a gimmick
- **Be concise.** Cold emails should be 3-4 short paragraphs max. Get to the ask quickly
- **Sound human, not corporate.** You're Charlie, founder. Write like a founder, not a marketing department
- **Show you know their business.** Reference their design philosophy, recent work, or partnership history
- **Make it easy to say yes.** Provide specific next steps and availability times

### Don't:
- **Use hyperbole.** No "revolutionary," "game-changing," or "disrupting interiors." Let the product speak
- **Be pushy.** Offer value, not ultimatums. If they're not interested, move on gracefully
- **Overcomplicate.** Explain the mechanic simply. It's: swipe card → pattern reveals. That's it
- **Generic praise.** "Love your brand!" is weak. Be specific about *why* and *how* it fits
- **Overpromise metrics.** Give realistic impression estimates and explain how they're calculated

## Quick Email Templates by Scenario

### If They Ask "What's Your CAC?"
"We're seeing $0.80 CAC through Instagram, with strong organic discovery from micro-influencer recommendations. Word-of-mouth is primary growth engine right now."

### If They Ask "How Is This Different From a Banner Ad?"
"We're not interruptive. The wallpaper reveal happens naturally as part of the swipe mechanic—users are already engaged in product discovery. It's 2-3 seconds of attention that feels integrated, not intrusive. Plus, you get demographic data on who engages most, not just impression counts."

### If They Ask "What If We're Not Sure About the Product?"
"That's why the pilot exists. Three months lets you test, gather data, see how your audience responds, and decide if it makes sense long-term. Worst case: you got 3 months of data on brand affinity. Best case: you discover a new channel."

### If They Ask "Can We Create a Custom Pattern?"
"Absolutely. For Signature and Featured partners, we can work with you to develop custom patterns. We handle the technical side; you own the creative. Typical turnaround is 2-3 weeks."
