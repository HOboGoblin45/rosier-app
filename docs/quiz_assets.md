# Rosier Style Quiz - Image Assets Guide

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Created:** April 2026
**Purpose:** Specification for 20 style quiz images to be sourced, shot, or designed

---

## Overview

The Rosier onboarding quiz consists of 5 questions with 4 visual options each, requiring **20 total images**. This document provides detailed specifications for each image set to guide designers, photographers, or asset sourcing.

**Key Requirements:**
- All images must reflect niche/designer fashion aesthetic
- Consistent photography style and lighting
- Authentic, high-quality visual representation
- Diversity in representation and body types
- Clear, uncluttered backgrounds (white or neutral)
- Optimized for mobile (square or near-square crops)
- Final resolution: 400×400px (app display) + 800×800px (2x retina)

---

## Question 1: What Catches Your Eye?

**Type:** Silhouette / Garment Structure
**Theme:** How items are constructed and fit the body

### Q1 Option 1: Structured & Tailored

**Visual Direction:**
- Tailored blazer, defined waistline, sharp shoulders
- Crisp seams, precision tailoring visible
- Model: wearing well-fitted, structured garment
- Example brands: Lemaire, Khaite, Thom Browne
- Color palette: Warm neutrals (taupe, camel, chocolate)

**Asset Specifications:**
- Image type: Fashion photograph of structured blazer on model
- Key visual: Clear jacket silhouette with defined waistline
- Background: White or light neutral
- Model: Any gender, any skin tone (show diversity)
- Pose: Front-facing or 3/4 view to emphasize structure
- Recommended sources: SSENSE, Browns Fashion product images

### Q1 Option 2: Relaxed & Effortless

**Visual Direction:**
- Oversized button-up, loose silhouette, flowing fabric
- Soft folds in fabric, casual drape
- Model: in oversized/relaxed fit clothing
- Example brands: Sandy Liang, Baserange, Khaite
- Color palette: Warm creams, soft grays

**Asset Specifications:**
- Image type: Fashion photograph of oversized shirt/dress on model
- Key visual: Relaxed fit, visible fabric drape and movement
- Background: White or neutral
- Model: Casual posture, relaxed stance
- Pose: 3/4 or profile to show silhouette flow
- Recommended sources: SSENSE lookbooks, Editorial content

### Q1 Option 3: Body-Conscious & Fitted

**Visual Direction:**
- Form-fitting dress or top, emphasizes natural curves
- Stretchy or contoured fabric
- Model: in fitted, bodycon silhouette
- Example brands: The Row, Jacquemus, Khaite
- Color palette: Deep jewel tones or classic black

**Asset Specifications:**
- Image type: Fashion photograph of fitted dress/top on model
- Key visual: Fitted silhouette that shows body contours
- Background: White or neutral
- Model: Posture that emphasizes the fitted shape
- Pose: Front-facing to show body contours
- Recommended sources: Designer collection photography

### Q1 Option 4: Avant-Garde & Experimental

**Visual Direction:**
- Unconventional cuts, unexpected proportions, artistic details
- Asymmetrical hems, unexpected layering, bold experimentation
- Model: in bold, experimental fashion piece
- Example brands: Yohji Yamamoto, Rick Owens, Y/Project
- Color palette: Dramatic, bold colors or high-contrast blacks/whites

**Asset Specifications:**
- Image type: Fashion photograph of experimental/avant-garde piece
- Key visual: Unconventional silhouette, unexpected proportions
- Background: White or neutral (to isolate the piece)
- Model: Posture that emphasizes the piece's unique structure
- Pose: Angle that best showcases the design
- Recommended sources: Experimental designer lookbooks, SSENSE editorial

## Asset Sourcing Recommendations

### Option 1: Use Existing Retailer Product Images
- Source: SSENSE, Browns Fashion, Farfetch, Dover Street Market
- Advantage: Authentic brand photography, cost-effective, consistent quality
- Process: License existing product images from retailers
- Timeline: 2-3 weeks for licensing

### Option 2: Commission Original Photography
- Source: Fashion photographer specializing in product/lookbook photography
- Advantage: Branded consistency, control over styling, unique to Rosier
- Budget: $5,000–$15,000 for 20-piece shoot
- Timeline: 4-8 weeks including casting, shooting, post-production
- Recommended: Use for Q5 (Aesthetic Vibes) to maximize brand differentiation

### Option 3: Hybrid Approach
- Use retailer images for Q1–Q4 (factual, category-focused)
- Commission original photography for Q5 (lifestyle, aesthetic, brand voice)
- Budget: $3,000–$8,000
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks

## Technical Specifications

### Image Format & Dimensions
- **Display Resolution:** 400×400px (square, optimized for mobile)
- **Retina (2x):** 800×800px
- **File Format:** PNG or JPG
- **Color Space:** sRGB
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1 (square)

### Quality Standards
- **Sharpness:** Pixel-perfect at 400×400px
- **Compression:** Optimized for app (max 150KB per image at 400px)
- **Consistency:** Uniform color grading, lighting, contrast across all images
- **Accessibility:** High contrast, visible on mobile screens

### Editing Guidelines
- **Color Grading:** Consistent across all 20 images
- **Brightness:** Optimized for dark/light mode compatibility
- **Saturation:** Natural (no over-processing)
- **Cropping:** Focus on key subject, consistent white/neutral borders

---

## Diversity & Representation

**Requirement:** All images should reflect diverse body types, skin tones, and genders where models are used.

### Guidelines
- **Body Types:** Include various body types (petite, tall, curvy, athletic, etc.)
- **Skin Tones:** Represent diverse ethnic backgrounds
- **Genders:** Assume fluid/inclusive gender presentation where applicable
- **Age:** Represent age diversity where possible (20s, 30s, 40s+)
- **Styling:** Reflect how different body types wear the same garment
