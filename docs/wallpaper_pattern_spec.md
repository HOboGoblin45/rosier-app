# Rosier Wallpaper Pattern Asset Specification

## Overview

This document defines the four signature wallpaper houses and their curated patterns that will be revealed beneath Rosier's swipe-away product cards. Each wallpaper house represents a distinct aesthetic archetype aligned with Rosier user personalities.

**Design Principle:** Wallpapers function as a subtle luxury detail—rendered at 5-8% opacity as background texture impressions rather than full reproductions. These patterns create a tactile, high-end feel that reinforces the brand's positioning in elite design discovery.

---

## 1. DE GOURNAY — Classical Elegance & Chinoiserie Refinement

**House Overview:**
de Gournay specializes in hand-painted chinoiserie wallpapers, a design tradition rooted in 18th-century European fascination with Chinese aesthetics. Each panel requires up to 100 hours of hand-painting by multiple artists.

**Target Archetype:** Classic Refined
Users who appreciate timeless sophistication, heritage craftsmanship, and narrative-driven design. They seek patterns that tell stories of distant gardens and birds.

### Pattern 1: Earlham
- **Visual Description:**
  Delicate branches adorned with small blossoms, pheasants in flight, and subtle foliate scrollwork. The composition suggests a serene bird sanctuary with intricate botanical detail. Fine linework dominates with a sparse, meditative quality.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#1B3B4D` (deep teal-blue)
  - Secondary: `#D4AF37` (warm gold leaf accents)
  - Tertiary: `#E8D4B8` (cream)
- **Dark Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#0F1F2E` (charcoal-blue)
  - Secondary: `#F4D89F` (luminous gold)
  - Tertiary: `#3A4C56` (slate)
- **Pattern Type:** Scenic with ornithological detail
- **Mood Keywords:** Meditative, Heritage, Intricate, Ethereal, Timeless
- **Color Inspiration:** Historic Williamsburg blue dyed silk

### Pattern 2: Portobello
- **Visual Description:**
  Gnarled tree branches laden with clustered flowering vines, birds perched among blooms, layered foliage creating depth. The movement suggests wind through a private garden. Ornate but restrained.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#4A7C8C` (slate-blue)
  - Secondary: `#C9A961` (aged gold)
  - Tertiary: `#F5F1E8` (ecru)
- **Dark Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#2C4D57` (deep slate)
  - Secondary: `#E5C494` (warm bronze)
  - Tertiary: `#5A7C88` (dusty blue-grey)
- **Pattern Type:** Botanical with scenic composition
- **Mood Keywords:** Romantic, Whimsical, Garden, Nostalgic, Refined
- **Color Inspiration:** Blue-grey dyed silk from Portobello's original colorway

### Pattern 3: Chatsworth
- **Visual Description:**
  A more densely populated composition featuring pavilions nestled among towering trees, delicate bridges crossing water, exotic birds in repose. Landscape perspective suggests depth and pavilion architecture. Captures the romanticism of distant Cathay.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#2C5F7C` (prussian blue)
  - Secondary: `#D9A649` (deep gold)
  - Tertiary: `#F9F6F0` (alabaster)
- **Dark Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#1A3A4D` (near-black blue)
  - Secondary: `#E8C494` (luminous bronze)
  - Tertiary: `#5A7A8C` (cool grey-blue)
- **Pattern Type:** Scenic landscape with architecture
- **Mood Keywords:** Majestic, Escapist, Architectural, Exotic, Opulent
- **Color Inspiration:** Mercury grey Williamsburg with special bespoke colorway

### Pattern 4: St. Laurent
- **Visual Description:**
  Abstract flowering branches with willowy foliage, small birds in contemplative poses, water references suggested by curved linework. A more contemporary interpretation of traditional chinoiserie, with modernist restraint.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#3D6A7D` (teal-grey)
  - Secondary: `#B8956A` (soft bronze)
  - Tertiary: `#F7F3EE` (parchment)

  - Primary: `#25404F` (deep charcoal-blue)
  - Secondary: `#D4B896` (warm taupe-gold)
  - Tertiary: `#6A8A9D` (slate-blue)
- **Pattern Type:** Abstract botanical
- **Mood Keywords:** Contemporary, Understated, Sophisticated, Flowing, Poetic

## 2. PHILLIP JEFFRIES — Artisanal Naturalism & Textural Depth

**House Overview:**
Phillip Jeffries (est. 1976) pioneered luxury grasscloth wallcoverings and natural fiber textiles. Products are handcrafted using hemps, jutes, grasses, and raffias, laminated at artisanal mills in Japan.

**Target Archetype:** Minimalist Modern + Relaxed Natural
Users who favor understated elegance, organic materials, and tactile authenticity. They appreciate the craftsmanship inherent in natural fibers and seek refuge in texture-driven design.

### Pattern 1: Heritage Hemp
- **Visual Description:**
  Horizontal woven texture with irregular fiber visibility suggesting raw, hand-processed hemp strands. Subtle vertical ribbing creates movement. Mimics the visual rhythm of traditional hand-weaving with a focus on honest material presence.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#D4C5B9` (warm linen)
  - Secondary: `#E8DCC8` (lighter flax)
  - Tertiary: `#C2B0A0` (soft taupe)
- **Dark Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#8B7E6F` (deep khaki)
  - Secondary: `#A89680` (warm grey-brown)
  - Tertiary: `#5E564C` (charcoal-taupe)
- **Pattern Type:** Textural, woven fiber
- **Mood Keywords:** Artisanal, Organic, Honest, Grounded, Naturalist
- **Color Inspiration:** Natural hemp fiber colorway

### Pattern 2: Graceful Grass
- **Visual Description:**
  Fine, parallel vertical striations suggesting dried grass strands bound together. Irregular spacing creates visual rhythm. At texture scale: hairline striations with occasional thicker accent threads, capturing the irregular-yet-harmonious quality of hand-weaving.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#D9CFC7` (pale sage-tan)
  - Secondary: `#E5D9CB` (cream-wheat)
  - Tertiary: `#C7B8A8` (warm beige)
- **Dark Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#7A7366` (dark khaki)
  - Secondary: `#9D938B` (warm grey)
  - Tertiary: `#5A5047` (charcoal-brown)
- **Pattern Type:** Textural, linear fiber
- **Mood Keywords:** Serene, Organic, Calming, Restrained, Meditative
- **Color Inspiration:** Grasscloth in neutral wheat tones

### Pattern 3: Refined Raffia
- **Visual Description:**
  Irregular woven texture with thicker, bundled fiber strands creating visual depth. Weave pattern suggests hand-interlacing with occasional breaks in the grid, reminiscent of traditional raffia basketry. Adds tactile luxury.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#D8C8B8` (warm ivory-tan)
  - Secondary: `#E8DCC8` (light wheat)
  - Tertiary: `#BFA892` (soft brown)
- **Dark Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#9B8B7B` (warm taupe)
  - Secondary: `#A89680` (grey-brown)
  - Tertiary: `#6B5F52` (deep brown-grey)
- **Pattern Type:** Textural, woven fiber
- **Mood Keywords:** Sophisticated, Handcrafted, Warm, Inviting, Luxe-minimal
- **Color Inspiration:** Handwoven raffia in natural honey tones

### Pattern 4: Natural Jute
- **Visual Description:**
  Dense, tightly woven texture with uniformly defined crossing points. Mimics traditional jute fabric with a slightly coarser appearance than hemp or grass. Suggests durability and artisanal precision. Fine crosshatch pattern at scale.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#C9BAA8` (soft beige-tan)

  - Tertiary: `#5F564A` (charcoal-brown)
- **Pattern Type:** Textural, woven fiber
- **Mood Keywords:** Durable, Honest, Elemental, Understated, Grounded

## 3. SCHUMACHER — Exuberant Maximalism & Bold Heritage Prints

**House Overview:**
F. Schumacher & Co. (est. 1889) is known for historic prints, block prints, and bold botanical designs. Their Exuberant Prints collection features vibrant, large-scale patterns inspired by archival artwork and global sources.

**Target Archetype:** Eclectic Creative
Users who celebrate color, pattern layering, and artistic expression. They see design as storytelling and aren't afraid of visual boldness or competing patterns.

- **Visual Description:**
  A Chinese dragon winds sinuously through oversized chrysanthemums and peonies in an exuberant block-print style. Inspired by 1920s Art Deco era design, the pattern combines graphic dragon silhouettes with lush florals. Bold, unapologetic composition with strong visual hierarchy.
- **Light Mode Colors:**

- **Mood Keywords:** Exuberant, Artistic, Theatrical, Bold, Heritage-inspired

## 4. SCALAMANDR É — Zoological Drama & Iconic Silhouettes

**House Overview:**
Scalamandré (est. 1929) is celebrated for iconic zoological patterns. The legendary Zebras pattern (introduced 1945 at Gino's restaurant in Manhattan) remains their signature. Patterns feature graphic animal silhouettes rendered in rich, jewel-toned colorways.

**Target Archetype:** Bold Avant-Garde
Users who make fearless design statements, embrace animal motifs as symbolic luxury, and appreciate historical design prestige. They see pattern as personality.

### Pattern 1: The Iconic Zebras
- **Visual Description:**
  Graphic zebra silhouettes arranged in a dynamic, cascading pattern. Bold, confident striping rendered as solid shapes with high contrast. Each zebra is in a different pose suggesting movement. Pattern suggests both elegance and wild energy—a paradox of control and untamed spirit. Born in 1945 from the famous Manhattan restaurant Gino's.
- **Light Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#1A1A1A` (deep black)
  - Secondary: `#F5F5F0` (cream-white)
  - Tertiary: `#4A7C3C` (deep forest green) — optional jewel tone underlay
  - Accent: `#D4AF37` (burnished gold)
- **Dark Mode Colors:**
  - Primary: `#F5F5F0` (cream-white, reversed)
  - Secondary: `#2A2A2A` (near-black)
  - Tertiary: `#7AA85A` (warm sage)
  - Accent: `#F0C860` (warm gold)
- **Pattern Type:** Zoological
- **Mood Keywords:** Iconic, Bold, Historic, Energetic, Luxurious
- **Color Inspiration:** Classic black/cream with optional colorway overlays (yellow, denim, red, charcoal)

### Pattern 2: Leaping Cheetah

## Implementation Guidelines

### Opacity & Scaling
- **Base Opacity:** 5-8% for subtle background impression
- **Scale Factor:** Subtle patterns (naturals) at 1.2-1.5x scale; Bold patterns (florals, zoological) at 0.8-1.0x scale to maintain legibility
- **Blending Mode:** Screen (light mode) / Multiply (dark mode)

### Archetype Mapping
- **Minimalist Modern** → Phillip Jeffries (Heritage Hemp, Graceful Grass)
- **Eclectic Creative** → Schumacher (Chiang Mai Dragon, Citrus Garden, Josef Frank)
- **Classic Refined** → de Gournay (Earlham, Portobello, Chatsworth)
- **Bold Avant-Garde** → Scalamandré (Zebras, Leaping Cheetah, Tigre)
- **Relaxed Natural** → Phillip Jeffries (Refined Raffia, Natural Jute)

### Design System Integration
All patterns should be integrated into the Rosier design system with:
- Dynamic color switching for light/dark mode
- Opacity adjustments for readability over product cards
- Pattern scale adjustments based on device size
- Smooth transitions as users swipe cards away
